Steamed Lobster Tails


    • Time: 10 min
    • Serves: 4



    Ingredients Quantity
    tablespoon sea salt 1
    cup butter, melted 1/2
    lobster tails 4 (6 ounce)

    Step 1: Pour about 1 inch of water in the bottom of a large pot and bring to a boil.

    Step 2:Add the salt and place a steamer insert inside the pot so that it is just above the water level.

    Step 3:Put the lobster tails on the rack and cover the pot. Cover and steam for 8 minutes. And don't peek! Serve with melted butter.

Maple Salmon


    • Time: 60 min
    • Serves: 4



    Ingredients Quantity
    cup maple syrup 1/4
    teaspoon garlic salt 1/4
    tablespoons soy sauce 2
    teaspoon ground black pepper 1/8
    clove garlic, minced 1
    pound salmon 1


    Step 1: In a small bowl, mix the maple syrup, soy sauce, garlic, garlic salt, and pepper.

    Step 2:Place salmon in a shallow glass baking dish, and coat with the maple syrup mixture. Cover the dish, and marinate salmon in the refrigerator 30 minutes, turning once.

    Step 3:Preheat oven to 400 degrees F (200 degrees C).

    Step 4:Place the baking dish in the preheated oven, and bake salmon uncovered 20 minutes, or until easily flaked with a fork.

To Die For Fettuccine Alfredo


  • Time: 30 min
  • Serves: 6


Ingredients Quantity
ounces dry fettuccini pasta 24
dash garlic salt 1
cup butter 1 (6 ounce)
pint heavy cream 3/4
up grated Parmesan cheese 1
grated Romano cheese 3/4


Step 1: Bring a large pot of lightly salted water to a boil. Add fettuccini and cook for 8 to 10 minutes or until al dente; drain.

Step 2:In a large saucepan, melt butter into cream over low heat. Add salt, pepper and garlic salt. Stir in cheese over medium heat until melted; this will thicken the sauce.

Step 3:Add pasta to sauce. Use enough of the pasta so that all of the sauce is used and the pasta is thoroughly coated. Serve immediately.
